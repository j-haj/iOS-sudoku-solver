Introduction

Class that handles puzzle storage and solving implementations

Discussion

Sudoku puzzles are stored in column-major order as one long vector. This means that as you look at a Sudoku puzzle and index the cells, starting from the upper left corner of the puzzle, the indices increase as you follow each column down. When you reach the bottom of a column, the next index is the top row of the next column. Below is a diagram of a puzzle and its cell index layout:

[0][ 9][18][27][36][45][54][63][72]

[1][10][19][28][37][46][55][64][73]

[2][11][20][29][38][47][56][65][74]

[3][12][21][30][39][48][57][66][75]

[4][13][22][31][40][49][58][67][76]

[5][14][23][32][41][50][59][68][77]

[6][15][24][33][42][51][60][69][78]

[7][16][25][34][43][52][61][70][79]

[8][17][26][35][44][53][62][71][80]

A puzzle is decomposed into 9 3x3 blocks (referred to as indexBlocks in the class) and are labeled as follows:

[0][0][0][3][3][3][6][6][6]

[0][0][0][3][3][3][6][6][6]

[0][0][0][3][3][3][6][6][6]

[1][1][1][4][4][4][7][7][7]

[1][1][1][4][4][4][7][7][7]

[1][1][1][4][4][4][7][7][7]

[2][2][2][5][5][5][8][8][8]

[2][2][2][5][5][5][8][8][8]

[2][2][2][5][5][5][8][8][8]

When storing a puzzle, the value 0 denotes an unknown value.
